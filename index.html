Skyline Countries and Reflection Countries

What?

Consider comparing the countries by their area and the highest elevation. If the geography was distributed randomly, larger countries would have higher probability to have the higher peaks while the smaller countries would be unlikely to have them. And in general that's true on Earth, so let's focus on the outliers.

The ones whose elevation stands above their (size-wise) peers are called skyline countries here. And the reflection countries? Those are the ones opposite to skyline.

The general terms for the envelope entries on a plot is Pareto front. For example, Liechtenstein is a Pareto-efficient country: sure, you can find higher peaks than Grauspitz in the world, but to do that you must necessarily go to a larger country. If you go look at smaller countries you will only find lower points.

The reflection countries are the ones who have unusually low highest points for their size. For example all the other countries that are as big as Mauritania or larger all have higher peaks than Mauritania does. You might probably describe them as Pareto-effective in the opposite direction. Pareto-defective. Poor in height.
