Skyline Countries and Reflection Countries

What?

We compare the countries by their area and the highest elevation. If the geography was distributed randomly, larger countries would have higher probability to have the higher peaks while the smaller countries would be unlikely to have them. And in general that's true on Earth, so let's focus on the outliers.

The ones whose elevation stands above their (size-wise) peers are called skyline countries here. The Reflection? Those are the opposite ones.

The general terms for the envelope entries on a plot is Pareto front. For example, Liechtenstein is Pareto-efficient country. Sure, you can find higher peaks than Grauspitz in the world, but to do that you must necessarily go to a larger country. If you go look at smaller countries you will only find lower points.

The reflection countries are the ones who have unusually low highest points for their size. Such as Australia or Lithuania who have such low highest points that you'd expect to find in countries 10x smaller than them. You might probably describe them as Pareto-effective in the opposite direction. Pareto-defective.
